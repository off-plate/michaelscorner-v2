Your first hour with AI

The exact first hour I would walk a friend through. Five steps, one real task, no theory. Do it once and you will know what these tools are actually for.

1Step

Pick one tool

ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, and the free version is fine. Do not spend your first hour comparing tools, because for beginner tasks they are all good enough. Pick one and stay with it for a week.

2Step

Bring a real task

Skip test questions like "write me a poem." Bring something real from today: an email you owe someone, a long document you need to understand, a plan you keep postponing. You learn what AI is for by using it on your actual work.

3Step

Give it the whole picture

Write to it the way you would brief a colleague. Say who you are, what you need, and what the result should look like. Three full sentences of context will beat five keywords every time.

4Step

Push back on the draft

The first answer is a draft, and treating it as final is the most common beginner mistake. Tell it what is wrong and ask again, as many times as you need. Check every fact and number before you use it, because AI presents its guesses with full confidence.

5Step

Keep what worked

When a prompt gives you a good result, save it in your notes. Next time you paste it and change a few words instead of starting over. That habit is the entire secret behind the prompt library on this site.

That is the hour. When you want prompts already written this way, the library is next door.
