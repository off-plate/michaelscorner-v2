The Real Bill

Every project I ship gets an entry here: what I asked AI to do, what it got right, where it failed or I had to take over, and what the whole thing cost in hours and koruna. The failures stay in on purpose, including the projects where AI was honestly not worth it. A page of wins would read nicely and teach you nothing. The pattern only shows up when the receipts are honest: the first version arrives fast and feels like magic, and the last 20% lands on me. Each entry carries the date it was last checked, because these tools change monthly and a stale receipt is worth nothing.

Case/01 · Compass (personal finance app)Updated 07/2026

What I asked AI to do.

Build a personal finance app that holds my debts, my monthly budget, my goals, and a five-year plan in one place.

What it nailed.

The app itself. Screens, charts, the whole structure, running in the browser faster than I expected, and it survived round after round of my change requests without complaint.

Where I took over.

The math and the look. The first projections were optimistic in a way no real month would survive, so I checked every formula against my actual spending before trusting a single chart. And the first design looked like every AI dashboard on the internet, so I wrote hard design rules and had it start the visuals again.

Case/02 · FORGE (3D training site)Updated 07/2026

What I asked AI to do.

Turn my training plan into a website with a rotating 3D wireframe figure, the kind of display you would expect in a sci-fi film.

What it nailed.

The 3D. I cannot write Three.js and I did not have to. The wireframe human was spinning in my browser before I fully understood what Three.js even is, and that is the honest reason this project exists at all.

Where I took over.

Everything after the wow. Making text readable over a moving scene, calming the camera down, and getting the actual training plan into a shape I would follow took long rounds of me describing what looked wrong in plain words.

Case/03 · Italy trip plannerUpdated 07/2026

What I asked AI to do.

An app for a real ten-day road trip: a map of the stops, day-by-day plans, and expense splitting for two couples.

What it nailed.

The software. Working map, editable days, shared expenses. It replaced the usual spreadsheet and we used it on the actual trip.

Where I took over.

The facts. AI happily invented restaurants, prices, and drive times, so every real-world detail had to be checked by hand before it went into the plan. The code was the quick part, and the checking took far longer.

Case/04 · Tuck (Chrome extension)Updated 07/2026

What I asked AI to do.

A Chrome extension that hides all my other extensions behind one icon with a clean dropdown.

What it nailed.

The start. Manifest, permissions, a working list of my installed extensions, all produced quickly and mostly correct on the early tries.

Where it failed.

Chrome itself. Part of what I wanted is simply not allowed by Chrome's rules for extensions, and no prompt changes what the browser permits. Worse, AI kept proposing confident workarounds that could never work, and I spent real evenings on them before accepting the limit. The shipped version does less than the one I imagined.

Case/05 · Spotify Vibes (prompt to playlist)Updated 07/2026

What I asked AI to do.

A small web app where you type a sentence like "rainy Sunday drive" and get a Spotify playlist to match.

What it nailed.

The fun part. Turning a mood into search terms and songs worked early, and showing it to people never gets old.

Where I took over.

Everything around Spotify. Login, API keys, permissions, and the rules of someone else's platform ate more time than the whole AI part, and that is the piece no demo video ever shows.

Case/06 · This websiteUpdated 07/2026

What I asked AI to do.

Build this site. The prompt library, the tools, the pages you are reading right now.

What it nailed.

Volume. Pages, search, copy buttons, layout changes, rebuilt as many times as I asked. Without AI this site would still be an idea in a notes app.

Where I took over.

Taste. Left alone, AI designs the same site it designs for everyone else, and the early drafts proved it. I ended up writing a file of design rules it must follow, and the difference between draft one and this page is that file plus a lot of "no, try again."
