Case/01 / Tuck (Chrome extension)

What I asked AI to do.

Build a Chrome extension that hides all my other extensions behind one icon with a clean dropdown.

What it nailed.

The start. Manifest, permissions, and a working list of my installed extensions, produced quickly and mostly right on the early tries.

Where it failed.

Chrome itself. Part of what I wanted is simply not allowed by Chrome's rules for extensions, and no prompt changes what the browser permits. AI kept proposing confident workarounds that could never work, and I spent real evenings on them before accepting the limit.

Real timebeing counted, lands soon
Real costbeing counted, lands soon
Mixed

It shipped, but AI let me chase an impossible version far longer than an experienced developer would have.

Case/02 / Recall (macOS workspace launcher)

What I asked AI to do.

Build a small macOS app that restores my whole desktop in one click, opening the apps and files I use and snapping the windows into place.

What it nailed.

The launcher. Saving a set of apps and files and reopening them all with one click worked early, in Swift I could not have written on my own.

Where it failed.

The window snapping. macOS keeps window and Space control behind system protections, so arranging the windows for me is not allowed the way I pictured it. AI kept suggesting approaches the OS blocks, and the shipped version launches the workspace but does not lay it out.

Real timebeing counted, lands soon
Real costbeing counted, lands soon
Mixed

The launcher is genuinely useful. The part I most wanted, snapping the windows into place, is the part macOS will not let it do.
